Game Specification: Wild and Wanderful: A Cozy Adventure
Wild and Wanderful is a mobile-first, offline-first RPG designed for portrait play. It combines the tactical depth of a turn-based roguelike with a "cozy-adventure" aesthetic inspired by Studio Ghibli and classic fantasy like The Hobbit
------------------------------

1. Core Concept & Narrative

* The World: A randomly generated grid-based world (default 20x20, adjustable by the player). It features diverse biomes: forests, meadows, rivers, lakes, roads, and villages.
* The Quest: A "North Star" mission represented by a distant "X" on a blank map. The goal is to save the world/village, but the player is free to explore in any direction and is never forced to complete the mission.
* The Legacy: Upon completing the quest or choosing to retire, characters are added to a Leaderboard. These retired heroes populate the starting village or appear as rare NPCs/Ghosts in future playthroughs to aid the player.

1. Character & Combat System

* Classless Progression: Players do not pick a class. Instead, they define their playstyle through Equipped Items and Active Skills.
* Starting Heirlooms: A narrative intro with an NPC allows the player to choose one of three randomly generated starting weapons: Sword (Melee), Bow (Ranged), or Magic Wand (Magic/Environmental).
* Action Point (AP) Economy: All actions (moving, attacking, searching) cost AP. Managing these points is the core of tactical combat.
* The "Wound" System: Instead of HP, players have one Wound Slot per level.
* Resting: Recovers wounds but takes time and carries a risk of a "Surprise Attack."
* Tactical Swapping: Changing items or active skills in combat is a high-stakes move that consumes an entire turn (all AP).

1. Skill Tree & Active Abilities

* Item-Locked Skills: To use a specific skill (e.g., Power Strike), the required item (e.g., Sword) must be equipped.
* Limited Slots: Players can unlock many skills but may only have a limited number "Active" at once. Higher levels unlock additional active slots.
* Skill Categories:
* Defensive: e.g., Auto-dodge the first attack after a rest.
  * Offensive: e.g., Multi-target attacks or attacks that Daze enemies.
  * Utility: Increased gold find, or Charm/Disarm skills that allow enemies to yield peacefully.

1. Immersive Exploration & UX

* One-Thing-at-a-Time UX: A clean, immersive interface focusing on one view at a time to prevent clutter.
* Scene View: The default view. Shows a rich text description of the current location and "Peripheral Glimpses" (short descriptions of terrain in the North, East, South, and West).
* Interactive Map: The map is a physical item in the inventory. When opened, it displays the 20x20 grid, the player's path, and the quest marker. Players move by selecting an adjacent square on this map.
* Advanced Searching: The Search action (1 AP) can reveal Hidden Paths through impassable terrain (swamps, cliffs, thickets), creating shortcuts or escape routes.

1. Technical & Visual Style

* Platform: Optimized for one-handed mobile play.
* Connectivity: 100% Offline-first.
* Aesthetic: A "Cozy-Roguelike" feel with cute animal protagonists (Badgers, Red Pandas, Owls) and a soft, tactile art style (felt, papercraft, or watercolour).
